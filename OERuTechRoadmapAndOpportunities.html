OERu Tech Roadmap & Strategic Opportunities

Dave Lane (@lightweight)

OERu 16.10 Meeting
University of the Highlands and Islands
Inverness, Scotland - 3-5 October 2016

Single Sign-On (SSO)

Removing barriers to participation.

It's not a straightforward thing to achieve, but we're going to make it happen. Our goal is when a learner or an educator signs up to one of our services, they can use the same credentials to log into all of our services to remove hassles. Benefits us, too, because it reduces complexity in the long run and the administrative overhead of account access support requests.

For those interested, we're currently pursuing a solution based on the OAuth2 authorisation protocol, using our multi-site WordPress Course site as the Authenticaion source.

Analytics

Tracking the usage patterns of OERu technologies, especially quizzes and assessements.

With any innovation, it's crucial to determine in advance what "success" looks like, and then build into your product the ability to measure its performance so you can determine whether you meet that threshold.

We're working with a company called Lunametrics to integrate into each of our Course sites so that the way users interact with any quizzes included in those courses is recorded (anonymously) and the results can be reviewed allowing us to continually improve the quality of quizzes, their layout, and the questions and answers (both correct and incorrect).

Automated assessment services

Crowd sourced "question bank": proven questions (and answers).

Investigate adopting TAO (Open Assessment Technology)

The OERu is already coordinating the efforts of educators to develop Open Educational Resources. Why not ensure that quizzes and assessments created in conjunction with those resources become part of an integrated question bank, suitably categorised to make them searchable and easily recruited into other formulations of assessments. Over time, if we aggregate questions - especially if some of our courses involve learners designing new questions and answers - we can create a valuable resource for online assessments.

Online assessment services represent a major opportunity in tertiary educatio. At the moment these services are dominated by proprietary monopolies, so they're a great candidate for a more open, pan sector approach. We think these online question banks could be an extension of WikiEducator. We're also evaluating open source delivery platforms like TAO (Online Assessment Technologies). The beauty of open source is the ease of trialing solutions and being able to discard (without buyer's remorse, those which fail to live up to their promise), or help to build up those which show potential, and to rapidly scale up, with minimal cost or risk, those which demonstrate clear value.
