OERu Open Tech Toolset

Dave Lane (@lightweight)

Open Source Technologist, OER Foundation

OERu 16.10 Meeting, University of Highlands and Islands
Inverness, Scotland - 3-5 October 2016

Our Technology Stack (or Toolset) for the OERu Minimum Viable Product combines a collaborative authoring platform, wikieducator.org - built on the same technology underlying Wikipedia - and a delivery platform, course.oeru.org - built on WordPress.

Our Platform also includes collaboration and communication tools to engage both educators and learners. Moreover, we are also incorporating analytical tools to help us measure the effectiveness of various practices we adopt.

OERu Minimum Viable Product

An accredited full year course of study

How will it work?

Step 1: Educators assemble OERs in WikiEducator.org

OERu partners contribute course materials to Wikieduator, and work collaboratively with repesentatives of other partners. They can incorporate rich content [Rich text editing environment] and assessments [quiz] directly in the Wiki. All work is protected by version control [revisions]. It can be edited from anywhere, with any Internet-connected computer or device.

Step 2: Educators design Courses for independent study using OERu tools

Educators can plan and contribute to individual courses - and new collaborators brought up-to-speed - coordinating tasks through our "Kanban" planning board [Wekan screenshot], through email mailing lists [Groups screenshot] and their archives, and in our searchable Community forum [Community screenshot]. They can collaborate in real-time with other educators and us through our Chat environment [Rocketchat screenshot].

Step 3: Courses offered by OERu

Any partner can "harvest" any course at any time, creating a "snapshottable" version for their institution.

At the click of a button, a Wikieductor course becomes a mobile-friendly WordPress site...

...either on the institution's own infrastructure or on our Course site [side-by-side screenshot of WE course -> WP course]. Of course, institutions can also choose reuse the course materials by copying and pasting them into their desired form.

Step 4: Learners take Courses

Learners can sign up for courses, either through partner institutions, or by signing up on through OERu.org. This is particularly well suited to supporting "open boundary" courses. By registering, learners will be engaged with course-related instructional emails. These communications will be delivered through our learner engagement platform, Mautic, which will allow us to determine when learners act upon these emails. [CSF email screenshot]

Step 5: Learners gain perspective by sharing

Learners can share and learn with one another though WENotes directly [screenshot], and via communication pulled from a wide variety of other places - suitably tagged posts on their own blogs, other social media (Twitter, Google+), the OERu's Rocket.Chat, the OERu mailing lists, and our learner Forums.

This is an example of WENotes filtered for a specific Course.

Step 6: Learners request assessment from partners

Branded course communications lead learners to partner assessment services and qualifications.

Finally, upon completing a course, learners can be assessed by whatever means is deemed appropriate by the hosting partner institution, and earn credit upon meeting the assessment threshold.

Our toolset is entirely Open Source

Walking the "Open" talk

All of our tools are open source software. For us at the OER Foundation, it's about cultural alignment and consistency with our open principles - we're not just talking about being open.

For Partners...

Try before you (don't) buy

Adapt quickly and decisively

Remove barriers

Economies of scale without being small fish

For our partners, its an opportunity to experience and use tools that don't get aggressively marketed to you. The ones that work well for you are there for the taking. You can cherry pick the ones you like best, and with our assistance, adopt them into your institutions. Also, with open source tools, you benefit from economies of scale - these tools all have international user bases, but without being treated like a small fish in a big pond. Plus, each one you adopt will likely save you far more than your OERu membership fees.

For Learners...

Access and empowerment

Reversible choices

Uncompromised freedom

For our learners it means accessibility and empowerment - they cannot be denied access of tools for lack of money for proprietary licenses, nor are they forced to sacrifice their freedoms in technology choice. Not only can learners *use* these tools to broaden their horizons, they're also encouraged to learn how they work, and make them even more fit-for-purpose.

Analytics

Designed to measure OERu effectiveness

We are increasingly building analytics tools like Piwik and Mautic into all of our services, so that, for example, course websites and associated email communication with students will be handled by systems that can measure the results of those communications. We want to be gathering information necessary to ensure the OERu is constantly learning, adapting, and improving.
